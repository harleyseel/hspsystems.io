About HSP

Built Inside
Real Businesses.

HSP Systems was not prototyped in a lab or assembled from generic agency templates. It was built from the ground up inside operating service companies — then refined into a repeatable system for businesses that need real operational infrastructure.

The Origin Story

HSP Systems started at Expert Cleaning Services — a service business in West Michigan with three core service lines the engine had to support: residential cleaning, commercial cleaning, and post-construction cleaning. Harley Seelbinder was brought in to build the operational backbone behind those services — lead capture, follow-up automation, hiring automation, HR workflows, review systems, quoting logic, and QuickBooks-connected processes.

Because the business served multiple service types, the system had to do more than collect leads. It had to route the right inquiries, support different intake paths, handle different job types, and create operational consistency behind the scenes. What began as an internal operations build quickly became something bigger.

Then the model was deployed at Wenke Landscaping, where the same systems thinking was adapted across multiple service lines. Different industry, different workflow — same result: cleaner routing, faster response, stronger follow-up, and less dependence on memory and manual admin work.

That is when HSP Systems became a product, not just a project. Every system we sell has been built, tested, and refined in real businesses before it ever reaches a client.

The Founder

HSP Systems was founded by Harley Seelbinder, an industrial/systems engineer and business operator based in West Michigan.

Harley's background is not in selling software for the sake of software. It is in studying how businesses actually run, finding where things break, and designing systems that remove bottlenecks. His work is grounded in operations, process design, automation, and measurable outcomes.

He has spent years inside service businesses — not above them. That matters. HSP was built by someone who understands what it feels like when leads go cold, hiring falls apart, admin work piles up, and growth creates chaos instead of momentum.

Built by Harley Seelbinder, an industrial/systems engineer who works inside real service businesses — not outside them.

That perspective shapes everything HSP builds. The goal is never to add more tools. The goal is to build infrastructure that makes the business easier to run, easier to scale, and less dependent on the owner being involved in every step.

HSP is based in Grand Haven, Michigan and works primarily with service businesses across West Michigan — though the systems themselves can be deployed anywhere.

How We Work

Principles, Not Pitches.

01
Build it in production first
We don't sell anything we haven't already deployed and stress-tested in a live business. If it didn't work inside Expert Cleaning or Wenke, it doesn't get sold to you.
02
Systems over software
Software is just the tool. The value is in the system design — the routing, automations, handoffs, logic, and workflows that move a lead from form submission to booked job.
03
Outcomes, not outputs
We don't measure success by how many workflows we build. We measure it by whether leads are getting answered, estimates are getting booked, and your business is running with less friction.
04
Modular by design
You shouldn't have to buy everything at once. Start with the problem that hurts most — lead response, IVR, hiring, reactivation — and expand when the business is ready.
05
Transparent pricing
Fixed setup fees. Clear monthly costs. No surprise invoices. No vague retainers. No scope-creep billing games. You know what you are paying before we start.
06
Say no more than yes
We only take on businesses we can genuinely help. If your problem doesn't match what we build, we'll tell you directly and point you in a better direction.
